Europa Universalis IV

Author: Simon Shoban

Date: 2017-04-08

Genre: Grand Strategy

I lied.

Now that you're here, you should check out Europa Universalis IV, it's great, that loser Sid Meier doesn't want you to know that.

Europa Universalis IV is a Grand Strategy game made by Paradox Interactive. At the start of the game, you can choose to play as any country in the world from 1444-1821. The vast majority of players start in 1444 because it is the most supported start date by Paradox and it gives you the most play time. Also, it's raining a lot right now, but you probably don't care about that.

This game has a lot of very complicated mechanics that are still very complicated despite three and a half years of the developers dumbing it down for us. But it mostly boils down to having more soliders and more money than everyone else. Mostly. Every nation has a ruler with randomly generated stats except at the start of the game where historical leaders are used and their stats are based off of their historical performance. The three stats are administrative, diplomatic, and military; they range from 0-6. These points are mostly used to increase your nation's level of technology and to "core" conquered lands.

So now you've picked your nation, then what? Well, that's up to you. Some people like to set a goal of conquering a certain piece of land, some people like to colonize, some people like to trade and makes lots of money, and some people just want to paint the map in their favourite colour. Fortunately, there are 225 EU4 achievements on Steam that can help you figure out what exactly you're working towards. One of the more difficult achievements is called Sunset Invasion; it requires you to conquer Lisbon, Madrid, Rome, Paris, Amsterdam, and London as the Aztec. You should buy it, preferably right now.
